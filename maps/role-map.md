# Role Map

## Purpose

A role map describes who is involved in a process and what each role is responsible for.

It helps make ownership and handoffs visible.

## When to Use

Use a role map when:

- multiple people or systems participate
- ownership is unclear
- handoffs matter
- the process crosses team boundaries

## Core Structure

### 1. Roles

List each role involved in the process.

### 2. Responsibilities

For each role, capture:

- primary responsibility
- inputs
- outputs
- decision authority
- handoff points

### 3. Coordination

Note where roles need to align or exchange information.

### 4. Risk

Identify where ambiguity, delay, or duplication can happen.

## Output Shape

A role map should usually include:

- role list
- responsibilities
- handoffs
- decision ownership
- coordination risks
