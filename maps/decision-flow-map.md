# Decision Flow Map

## Purpose

A decision flow map describes how a decision is made over time.

It helps clarify:

- decision criteria
- inputs
- branches
- tradeoffs
- ownership
- final outcome

## When to Use

Use a decision flow map when:

- a choice affects the rest of the process
- multiple conditions change the result
- the team needs consistent judgment
- the decision logic is currently unclear

## Core Structure

### 1. Decision

- what is being decided
- why the decision matters

### 2. Inputs

- what information is needed
- what constraints apply

### 3. Criteria

- what must be true
- what should be preferred
- what should be avoided

### 4. Branches

Map the decision paths.

### 5. Result

- what happens after each branch
- what the final action is

## Output Shape

A decision flow map should usually include:

- decision statement
- inputs
- criteria
- branches
- final result
- open questions
