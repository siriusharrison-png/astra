# Standards

## Scope

This file contains the stable conventions that should rarely change.

## Standards

- Keep content cross-project and reusable
- Keep structural rules separate from working notes
- Keep task-specific material out of the stable layer
- Keep terminology consistent across files and sessions
- Keep updates in English first
- Keep file names and section names short, explicit, and stable
- Keep the top-level vocabulary limited and intentional
- Keep the stable layer readable without extra explanation

## Change Policy

- Change carefully
- Confirm before modifying stable definitions
- Do not use this layer for temporary discussion
- If a change would alter the meaning of the foundation, pause and confirm first
- If a change only improves clarity without changing meaning, it may be added to the evolving layer

## Design Quality Dimensions

These dimensions describe what makes design decisions feel right across Astra work.
They are reference points, not checklist items. Use them in patterns, templates, and cross-project handoffs.

Do not use these as a rubric. They are vocabulary, not rules.

### Clarity

**What it means:** Information architecture is obvious at first read. The mental model forms immediately. 
Next steps are visible without searching. The intent is unambiguous.

**Not:** Everything visible at once. Over-simplified to the point of vagueness.

**When it matters:** Handoffs, spec writing, pattern definition. Clarity is essential when knowledge moves between people or phases.

---

### Refinement

**What it means:** Details are considered and intentional. Nothing feels rough, incomplete, or like an afterthought. 
Decisions are visible in how things are made, not hidden.

**Not:** Perfectionism. Every pixel polished. Pursuing finish over substance.

**When it matters:** Any output meant to be studied or reused. Refinement builds trust in the thinking behind a decision.

---

### Responsiveness

**What it means:** The system reacts to intent quickly and predictably. Feedback is immediate and unambiguous. 
Causality is clear: I did X, Y happened.

**Not:** Immediate gratification. Sacrificing accuracy for perceived speed. Feedback that obscures instead of clarifies.

**When it matters:** Workflow design, interaction patterns, async communication. Users and agents both need to know the state changed.

---

### Composability

**What it means:** Pieces fit together without friction. Modular but cohesive. 
One element works in different contexts; the system stays recognizable.

**Not:** Sameness everywhere. Rigid consistency. Forcing unrelated things into the same template.

**When it matters:** Pattern definition, template design, cross-project work. Composability lets insights move between projects without rewriting.

---

### Generativity

**What it means:** The work collects and connects ideas with deliberate purpose. It opens to many points (diverge), then weaves them into structure (converge).
Early stage: gather multiple perspectives, questions, and observations without rushing to closure.
Late stage: connect points with evidence—anchoring each to discussion, reference, or consideration. The final output (idea, goal, or structure) is grounded, not arbitrary.
The process stays open to new information without staying open forever.

**Not:** Collecting ideas endlessly without synthesis. Forcing premature closure. Building from a single perspective.

**When it matters:** Discovery and exploration, reference analysis, scope framing, pattern development. Generativity bridges from curiosity to actionable thinking.

---

### Criticality

**What it means:** The work maintains steady skepticism without hardness. It surfaces hidden assumptions, names what is uncertain, and asks "what works and what should we leave behind?"
It does not require deciding everything; it requires staying awake to what is working and what is not.
Held positions remain open to evidence; challenge is invited, not feared.

**Not:** Cynicism or dismissal. Harsh judgment. Paralysis by endless critique. Pretending certainty where there is none.

**When it matters:** Judgment work, pattern refinement, standards updates, architecture decisions. Criticality keeps thinking honest and adaptive, not brittle or defensive.
